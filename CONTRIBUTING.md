# Contributing Guide

## Welcome

First of all — thank you for being here.

This community is built around learning, sharing, and growing together.  
There is no pressure to be perfect or to contribute in a specific way.

If you're here, you're already contributing.

---

## How You Can Contribute

You can contribute in many different ways:

### 💻 Share your work
- Create and upload your own projects
- Keep them simple or complex — both are valuable
- Follow the base concept of the organization, but feel free to be creative

---

### 🔍 Explore and learn
- Check other members’ projects
- Study their code
- Talk with them about why have they implemented whatever intriges you 
- Use it as inspiration for your own learning

---

### 💬 Give feedback
- Suggest improvements for projects, but also improvements to our organization
- Help others debug or improve their code
- Share ideas in a respectful and constructive way

---

### 💡 Share ideas
- Suggest new project concepts
- Improve existing structures
- Help shape the community

---

## 📂 Project Guidelines

When adding a project:

- Keep your files organized
- Use clear naming for folders and files, remember: yourName/projectName
- Add a `README.md` explaining your project
- Stay aligned with the core concept of the organization
- Keep your work inside your folder to avoid conflicts with others.

---

## 🌿 Keep It Simple

This is not a competition.

- You don’t need to impress anyone
- You don’t need to contribute constantly
- You don’t need to know everything

Just build, learn, and share at your own pace.

---

## 🤝 Be Respectful

When interacting with others:

- Be kind and supportive
- Give constructive feedback
- Respect different skill levels
- Follow our [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

---

## 🔄 Basic Workflow (GitHub)

If you're contributing code to a collaborative-project:

1. Fork or clone the repository
2. Create a new branch  
   `feature/your-project-name`
3. Make your changes
4. Commit clearly  
   `Add: new project - recipe page`
5. Open a Pull Request

---

## ❗ Important Note

There are no strict rules on how much you should contribute.

Participation is optional.

What matters is:
- learning
- experimenting
- helping each other

---

## ❤️ Final Words

This space exists for you.

Take initiative, be creative, and don't be afraid to share your work — no matter your level.
